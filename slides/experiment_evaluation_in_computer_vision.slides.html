Accuracy¶

Accuracy is also used as a statistical measure of how well a binary classification test correctly identifies or excludes a condition. That is, the accuracy is the proportion of correct predictions (both true positives and true negatives) among the total number of cases examined. As such, it compares estimates of pre- and post-test probability. To make the context clear by the semantics, it is often referred to as the "Rand accuracy" or "Rand index". It is a parameter of the test.

$$ \textrm{Accuracy} = \frac{TP + TN}{TP + TN + FP + FN} $$

Sources: Metz1978 and Wiki: Accuracy and Precision

Precision, Recall=Sensitivity, Specificity¶

Precision (also called positive predictive value) is the fraction of relevant instances among the retrieved instances, while recall (also known as sensitivity) is the fraction of relevant instances that were retrieved. Both precision and recall are therefore based on relevance.

Recall, Sensitivity or True Positive Rate (TPR), is the proportion of people that tested positive and are positive (True Positive, TP) of all the people that actually are positive (Condition Positive, CP = TP + FN). It can be seen as the probability that the test is positive given that the patient is sick. With higher sensitivity, fewer actual cases of disease go undetected (or, in the case of the factory quality control, fewer faulty products go to the market).

Specificity (SPC) or True Negative Rate (TNR) is the proportion of people that tested negative and are negative (True Negative, TN) of all the people that actually are negative (Condition Negative, CN = TN + FP). As with sensitivity, it can be looked at as the probability that the test result is negative given that the patient is not sick. With higher specificity, fewer healthy people are labeled as sick (or, in the factory case, fewer good products are discarded).

ROC curve¶

ROC kivka (z anglickho Receiver Operating Characteristic, operan charakteristika pijmae) je graf, kter popisuje kvalitu binrnho klasifiktoru v zvislosti na nastaven jeho klasifikanho prahu.

Source: scikit-learn docs

m tedy je ROC kivka zakivenj ve smru vzhru, tm vc klasifikan promnn obsahuje informace o zaazen objekt a tm je jej pouit pro danou klasifikan lohu vhodnj. Jako souhrnn hodnocen klasifikan schopnosti promnn se proto asto pouv plocha pod ROC kivkou.

Binrnm klasifiktorem se rozum zazen (nebo lovk) odhadujc zaazen njakch objekt nebo ppad do dvou skupin, obecn oznaovanch jako pozitivn a negativn. Pitom se pedpokld, e objekty jsou charakterizovny njakou vlastnost (promnnou) vyjdenou slem a e klasifiktor zaazuje do pozitivn skupiny prv ty, u kterch hodnota tto promnn peshne stanoven klasifikan prh (co je njak pevn dan slo). Pkladem binrnho klasifiktoru me bt bank, kter na zklad kreditnho skre njak osoby rozhoduje, zda j lze (pozitivn) nebo nelze (negativn) spn poskytnout vr. Klasifiktor na zklad takovho postupu me bu objekt zaadit sprvn, anebo se me dopustit chyby; navc zle i na tom, zda ppad je, nebo nen ve skutenosti pozitivn. Lze tak rozliit tyi ppady: 1. skuten pozitivn (true positive, jejich poet se oznauje TP; odpovd situaci, kdy bank poskytne vr a klient ho dn splat), 2. skuten negativn (true negative, TN; bank odmtne klienta, kter by vr nesplatil), 3. falen pozitivn ili falen poplach (false positive, FP; poskytnut vru klientovi, kter penze bance nevrt) a 4. falen negativn (false negative, FN; klient nedostane vr, i kdy by ho dn splcel).

Regression¶

Regression is a process of finding the correlations between dependent and independent variables. It helps in predicting the continuous variables such as prediction of Market Trends, prediction of House prices, etc.

The task of the Regression algorithm is to find the mapping function to map the input variable(x) to the continuous output variable(y).

Source

Regrese je proces hledn korelac mezi zvislmi a nezvislmi promnnmi. Pomh pi pedpovdn spojitch promnnch, jako je predikce trnch trend, predikce cen dom atd.

kolem regresnho algoritmu je najt mapovac funkci pro mapovn vstupn promnn (x) na spojitou vstupn promnnou (y).

Regression vs Classification¶

Hlavn rozdl mezi regresnmi a klasifikanmi algoritmy je ten, e regresn algoritmy se pouvaj k predikci spojitch hodnot, jako je cena, plat, vk atd., a klasifikan algoritmy se pouvaj k predikci/klasifikaci diskrtnch hodnot, jako je mu nebo ena, pravda nebo nepravda, Spam or Not Spam atd.

Source: https://www.javatpoint.com/

Regresn algoritmus Algoritmus klasifikace
Pi regresi mus nabvat vstupn promnn spojitch hodnot. Pi klasifikaci mus bt vstupn promnn diskrtn hodnota.
kolem regresnho algoritmu je mapovat vstupn hodnotu (x) na spojitou vstupn promnnou (y). kolem klasifikanho algoritmu je mapovat vstupn hodnotu (x) na diskrtn vstupn promnnou (y).
Regresn algoritmy se pouvaj se spojitmi daty. Klasifikan algoritmy se pouvaj s diskrtnmi daty.
Pi regresi se sname najt nejvhodnj linii, kter doke pesnji pedpovdt vstup. Pi klasifikaci se sname najt rozhodovac hranici, kter me rozdlit datovou sadu do rznch td.
Regresn algoritmy lze pout k een regresnch problm, jako je pedpov poas, pedpov ceny domu atd. Klasifikan algoritmy lze pout k een problm klasifikace, jako je identifikace spamovch e-mail, rozpoznvn ei, identifikace rakovinnch bunk atd.
Regresn algoritmus lze dle rozdlit na linern a nelinern regresi. Algoritmy klasifikace lze rozdlit na binrn klasifiktor a klasifiktor s vce tdami.

Source: https://www.javatpoint.com/

Regression Algorithm Classification Algorithm
In Regression, the output variable must be of continuous nature or real value. In Classification, the output variable must be a discrete value.
The task of the regression algorithm is to map the input value (x) with the continuous output variable(y). The task of the classification algorithm is to map the input value(x) with the discrete output variable(y).
Regression Algorithms are used with continuous data. Classification Algorithms are used with discrete data.
In Regression, we try to find the best fit line, which can predict the output more accurately. In Classification, we try to find the decision boundary, which can divide the dataset into different classes.
Regression algorithms can be used to solve the regression problems such as Weather Prediction, House price prediction, etc. Classification Algorithms can be used to solve classification problems such as Identification of spam emails, Speech Recognition, Identification of cancer cells, etc.
The regression Algorithm can be further divided into Linear and Non-linear Regression. The Classification algorithms can be divided into Binary Classifier and Multi-class Classifier.

Mean Square Error¶

$$ \text{MSE}(y, \hat{y}) = \frac{1}{n_\text{samples}} \sum_{i=0}^{n_\text{samples} - 1} (y_i - \hat{y}_i)^2. $$

Mean Absolute Error¶

$$ \text{MAE}(y, \hat{y}) = \frac{1}{n_{\text{samples}}} \sum_{i=0}^{n_{\text{samples}}-1} \left| y_i - \hat{y}_i \right|. $$

Mean Absolute Error¶

$$ \text{Max Error}(y, \hat{y}) = max(| y_i - \hat{y}_i |) $$

Detection - Evaluation¶

Intersection over Union (Jaccard index)¶

$$ J(A,B) = {{|A \cap B|}\over{|A \cup B|}} = {{|A \cap B|}\over{|A| + |B| - |A \cap B|}} $$

Dice coefficient¶

$$ DICE = \frac{2\left|{S_{g}^{1}} \cap {S_{t}^{1}}\right|}{\left|{S_{g}^{1}}\right| + \left|{S_{t}^{1}}\right|} = \frac{2 TP}{2 TP + FP + FN} $$

Jaccard index is always larger than DICE except at the extrema {0,1} where they are equal. Furthermore the two metrics are related according to

$$ JAC=\frac{\left|{S_{g}^{1}} \cap {S_{t}^{1}}\right|}{\left|{S_{g}^{1}} \cup {S_{t}^{1}}\right|}=\frac{2\left|{S_{g}^{1}} \cap {S_{t}^{1}}\right|}{2\left(\left|{S_{g}^{1}}\right|+\left|{S_{t}^{1}}\right|- \left|{S_{g}^{1}} \cap {S_{t}^{1}}\right|\right)} =\frac{DICE}{2-DICE} $$

Segmentation - evaluation¶

Per pixel (voxel)

  • Jaccard index (IoU) $$ JAC=\frac{\left|{A} \cap {B}\right|}{\left|{A} \cup {B}\right|} $$
  • Dice coefficient $$ DICE = \frac{2\left|{A} \cap {B}\right|}{\left|{A}\right| + \left|{B}\right|} = \frac{2 TP}{2 TP + FP + FN} $$

  • Pixel Accuracy $$ \textrm{Accuracy} = \frac{TP + TN}{TP + TN + FP + FN} $$

    Pixel Accuracy can sometimes provide misleading results when the class representation is small within the image, as the measure will be biased in mainly reporting how well you identify negative case (ie. where the class is not present).

  • Hausdorf distance $$ d(\mathcal{S}_1, \mathcal{S}_2) = max_{p \in \mathcal{S}_1}(d(p, \mathcal{S}_2)) \label{eq:05} $$
  • Max distance $$ \text{MaxD} = max(d(\mathcal{S}_1, \mathcal{S}_2), d(\mathcal{S}_2, \mathcal{S}_1)) \label{eq:04} $$
  • Average Symmetric Surface Distance $$ \text{ASD}=\frac1{{\left|\text{S}{\left(\text{GT}\right)}\right|}+{\left|{\left.\text{S}\left(\text{MS}\right.\right)}\right|}}{\left(\sum_{\left.{\text{S}}_\text{gt}\in\text{S}\left(\text{GT}\right.\right)}\text{d}{\left({\text{S}}_\text{gt},\text{S}{\left(\text{MS}\right)}\right)}+\sum_{\left.{\text{S}}_\text{ms}\in\text{S}\left(\text{MS}\right.\right)}\text{d}{\left({\text{S}}_\text{ms},\text{S}{\left(\text{GT}\right)}\right)}\right)} $$ Average of all the distances from points on the boundary of MS to the boundary of the GT, and vice versa

Tracking - Evaluation¶

Point tracking

  • Mean Square Error
  • Hausdorff distance (MaxD)

Bounding box tracking:

  • Multiple Object Tracking Accuracy (MOTA) $$ MOTA = 1 - \frac{\sum_t{FN_t + FP_t + IDSt}}{\sum_t{GT_t}} $$ where $FN_t$ is the number of false negatives (missed targets), $FP_t$ the number of false positives (ghost trajectories), $IDS_t$ the number of identity switches at time t. A target is considered missed if the IoU with the ground truth is inferior to a given threshold. (Note that the MOTA can be negative.)
